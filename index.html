A quick message for your family

Our communities are at higher risk.

And most people don't know it.

Stomach cancer is much more common in Asian Americans
Asian American men get stomach cancer at more than twice the rate of white men. In Korean American communities, it can be up to 13 times more common. Most families have no idea.
It's especially common if you were born abroad
People who immigrated from Korea, Japan, China, or Vietnam get stomach cancer 2 to 3 times more often than Asian Americans born here. If your parents were born abroad, this applies to your family too.
Most cases are caught too late
Over 1 in 3 stomach cancers are only found after they've already spread. At that point, survival drops to under 8%. But when it's caught early ‚Äî most people survive.

This doesn't mean you will get it.
Higher risk just means it's worth paying attention to ‚Äî and there's an easy thing you can do right now.

There's a bacteria behind most of this.

And the good news ‚Äî it's treatable.

Meet H. pylori
Helicobacter pylori is a stomach bacteria that billions of people carry without knowing. No symptoms. No warning signs. But over many years, it can slowly damage your stomach and lead to cancer.
Testing and treating it is easy
A simple breath test, blood test, or stool test can tell you if you have it. If you do, a short course of antibiotics clears it up ‚Äî and lowers your cancer risk before anything serious happens.

Three simple steps. That's all.
